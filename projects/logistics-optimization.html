Optimizing Operation Costs for Logistics Centers

Integrated approach to vehicle routing and energy optimization in Seoul

This project addresses the operational challenges faced by urban logistics centers, combining vehicle routing optimization with renewable energy installation planning to minimize total operational costs.

Problem Context

Last-mile delivery in dense urban environments like Seoul presents unique challenges. Logistics centers must balance delivery efficiency, vehicle utilization, and increasingly, energy costs. With rising electricity prices and growing environmental concerns, integrating renewable energy sources becomes economically attractive.

We partnered with a logistics company operating multiple distribution centers in the Seoul metropolitan area to develop an integrated optimization framework.

Demand Simulation

Using historical delivery data and demographic information, we built a stochastic demand model for the Seoul region. Key features include:

  • Spatial clustering of delivery points by district
  • Temporal patterns capturing daily and weekly seasonality
  • Correlation structure between nearby regions

This model generates realistic demand scenarios for testing our optimization approaches.

Vehicle Routing

We formulated and solved Vehicle Routing Problems (VRP) and Traveling Salesman Problems (TSP) using Gurobi optimizer. Road network data from OpenStreetMap, processed with OSMnx, provides realistic travel times accounting for Seoul's complex urban geography.

Our formulation includes capacity constraints, time windows for commercial deliveries, and driver working hour regulations.

Energy Optimization

For each logistics center, we optimize the installation of photovoltaic (PV) panels considering:

  • Roof space availability and orientation
  • Local solar irradiance patterns
  • Time-of-use electricity pricing
  • Installation and maintenance costs
  • Government incentives and feed-in tariffs

Results

Our integrated approach achieved 12% reduction in total operational costs compared to the baseline. Vehicle routing improvements contributed 7%, while optimal PV installation provided an additional 5% through energy cost savings.

The solution has been partially implemented at two pilot centers, with full rollout planned for 2025.

Technical Stack

Python (NumPy, Pandas), Gurobi Optimizer, OSMnx, PVLIB for solar modeling, and custom visualization tools for route planning.
