Perturbation Analysis on Pre-trained SPO Framework

Establishing robust decision-making under distributional uncertainty

This project investigates the robustness of Smart Predict-then-Optimize (SPO) frameworks when subjected to perturbations in input data. We develop a two-stage training approach combined with distributionally robust optimization (DRO) to establish theoretical guarantees.

Motivation

The SPO framework bridges machine learning predictions with downstream optimization decisions. However, real-world applications face inevitable data perturbations—measurement noise, distribution shift, or adversarial manipulation. Understanding how these perturbations affect decision quality is crucial for deployment in critical systems.

Traditional approaches treat prediction and optimization separately, missing opportunities for end-to-end robustness. Our work addresses this gap by analyzing perturbation effects throughout the entire pipeline.

Methodology

We propose a two-stage training procedure:

  • Pre-training: Train a prediction model using standard SGD with traditional loss functions (MSE, cross-entropy)
  • Fine-tuning: Adapt the model using SPO+ loss, which directly measures decision quality

This approach combines the stability of traditional training with the decision-awareness of SPO methods. We then analyze robustness using tools from distributionally robust optimization.

Theoretical Contributions

Our main theoretical results include:

  • Tight bounds on decision regret under bounded perturbations
  • Characterization of the robustness-accuracy tradeoff
  • Conditions under which the two-stage approach provably outperforms end-to-end training

Experimental Results

We validate our approach on several benchmark problems including shortest path prediction, portfolio optimization, and resource allocation. Results demonstrate significant improvements in worst-case performance while maintaining competitive average-case accuracy.

Ongoing Work

Current extensions include handling non-convex optimization problems, developing computationally efficient robustness certificates, and applications to real-world supply chain optimization scenarios.
