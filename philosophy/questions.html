Questions I Keep Returning To

The questions that keep me up at night, the ones I find myself returning to again and again.

On Optimization

How do we make good decisions when we don't have complete information? This question underlies much of my work in optimization under uncertainty. The challenge isn't just computational. It's fundamental. What does it even mean to make a "good" decision when the future is unknown?

On Learning

When can we trust what we learn from data? Machine learning has achieved remarkable successes, but we still lack a complete understanding of when and why it works. The gap between empirical success and theoretical understanding fascinates me.

On Complexity

Why are some problems inherently hard? Computational complexity theory tells us that certain problems are intractable, but real-world instances are often solvable. Understanding this gap, between worst-case theory and typical-case practice, remains an open challenge.

On Simplicity

Why do simple methods often work so well? In optimization, in machine learning, in statistics, simple approaches frequently outperform complex ones. Is this a deep truth about the world, or just a reflection of our current understanding?

On Connection

How do different areas of mathematics connect? The most exciting discoveries often come from unexpected connections between disparate fields. I'm always looking for these bridges, these moments where techniques from one area illuminate problems in another.
